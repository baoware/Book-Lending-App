Welcome! Whether you're a Patron or a Librarian, here’s a simple walkthrough of how to use this website to browse, lend, or borrow books.

🏠 Home & Dashboard

Once you log in with your Google account, you’ll land on your dashboard. Your dashboard changes depending on your role:

  • Patrons see collections they can browse and items they can request to borrow.
  • Librarians see additional buttons to add, edit, or remove items and collections.
  • Your login also automatically creates a User Profile connected to your Google account.
  • You can add a profile picture and view your role (Patron or Librarian).

📬 Borrowing a Book (Patrons)

  • When viewing a book, if it’s available, you’ll see a “Request to Borrow” button.
  • Click the button to send your request to the librarians.
  • You’ll be notified once your request is approved or denied. - If approved, you’ll get instructions on when/where to pick up the item.

🔍 Browsing Books

Click “Collections” in the navigation bar to explore all available books:

You’ll see items that are:

  • Not part of any collection (like new arrivals!)
  • Inside public collections

You can also use the filter buttons to narrow down items by:

  • Genre (e.g., Mystery, Fantasy)
  • Status (Available, Checked out)
  • Condition (Like New, Good, etc.)

Use the search bar to find books by title.

Click on any book to view its details page, which includes:

  • The title, author, genre, and a description
  • The book’s status (available or checked out)
  • Ratings and comments from other users
  • A cover image (if provided)

🧺 Lending a Book (Librarians)

Librarians have an extra button in their dashboard called "Lend an Item".

  • Fill out the item form: title, author, condition, location, genre, etc.
  • Upload a cover image if you have one.
  • Submit to add the item to the library for others to see and request.

🗂️ Creating Collections

Patrons can create public collections (e.g., “Best Fantasy Books” or “Senior Year Favorites”).

Librarians can create both public and private collections:

  • Private collections are only visible to approved users.
  • Items in private collections can’t belong to any other collection.

Each collection includes:

  • A title and description
  • A list of books that belong to it
  • The creator’s name so others know who curated it

⭐ Rating and Commenting

After reading a book, you can:

  • Leave a star rating (1–5)
  • Add a comment or review for other users to read

This helps build community trust and makes the collection more helpful!

👤 Your Profile Page

Go to Profile in the navigation bar to:

  • View your account email and role
  • Upload or change your profile picture
  • See a list of books you’ve requested or borrowed

📦 Drop-off and Pickup Locations

When borrowing or lending an item, it’s important to know where the exchange happens. Our app is designed to make this as convenient and safe as possible. All items are exchanged at designated campus locations.

📍 Available Locations

When adding or browsing items, you’ll see one of the following approved drop-off/pickup locations listed:

  • Shannon Library
  • Student Health and Wellness
  • Gibbons
  • Rice Hall

Each book or item will show its location on its detail page.

🔁 Returning Items

Borrowers will be expected to return items to the same location they were picked up from, unless otherwise coordinated.

Librarians can mark items as “Returned” once they receive them back — this helps keep the library updated and organized.

🙋 For Patrons

1. Once your borrow request is approved, the item will be delivered to the specified location.

2. You'll receive a notification in your user profile for:

  • The location name
  • A brief description of the pickup procedure
  • Any time constraints (e.g., pickup time and due date)

3. Within 3 days of the item being due, you will receive a reminder notification.

🙋‍♀️For Librarians

When lending an item:

1. You must choose a pickup location from the dropdown when filling out the "Lend Item" form.

2. Make sure the item is dropped off on time and clearly marked with:

  • The title
  • The borrower's name
  • Any notes about return timing, if needed
